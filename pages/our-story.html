The Henderson He Remembers

Growing up, Henderson was alive.

There were parks everywhere. Nazkarts where Stephen and his friends would race on weekends. Adventure Island for birthday parties. The Skateeum where everyone hung out after school.

These weren't just places‚Äîthey were gathering points. Spots where families came together, where kids made memories, where the community felt like a community.

Then, slowly, they started disappearing. One by one, the places that brought Henderson together closed their doors. The parks got quieter. The memories got harder to make.

The Return

In 2024, Stephen came home.

Not to visit. Not to pass through. To rebuild.

He could have stayed in Ohio. He could have built something there. But Henderson needed someone who remembered what it used to be. Someone who could see what it could become.

Stephen saw two possible futures for his hometown: Either major corporations would eventually move in and reshape Henderson in their image. Or locals could band together and build something from the ground up.

He chose door number two.
